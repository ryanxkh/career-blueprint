# Career Blueprint — Jordan Rivera

## Career Goals

**1-Year Goal:**
Transition from Senior Marketing Director to a Head of Product Marketing role at a B2B SaaS company, focusing on the developer tools or data infrastructure space. Build credibility through hands-on GTM launches and measurable pipeline influence.

**3-Year Goal:**
Lead the full product marketing function (team of 4-6) at a Series B or C company, owning positioning, competitive intelligence, and product launch strategy. Be recognized as a go-to voice in the developer tools GTM space.

**5-Year Goal:**
Step into a VP of Marketing role with P&L influence, combining product marketing expertise with demand generation and brand strategy. Help scale a company from $20M to $100M ARR.

**10-15 Year Goal:**
Serve as CMO at a developer-focused company that I genuinely believe in, shaping how technical products are brought to market and building a marketing org that engineers actually respect.

**Ultimate Goal:**
Change how B2B companies communicate with technical buyers. Replace the jargon-heavy, feature-dumping playbook with honest, evidence-based marketing that helps people make better decisions. Leave the field better than I found it.

## Reflection & Alignment

**Why These Goals Matter:**
Marketing that respects the buyer's intelligence is rare in B2B, and I have seen firsthand how much revenue and trust gets left on the table when companies treat technical audiences like they are unsophisticated. I want to prove there is a better way, and I want to do it by building things, not just advising.

**Key Trade-offs Acknowledged:**
Moving from a Senior Director title at an established company to a Head of PMM at a smaller one likely means a step back in team size and possibly base comp. I am trading stability and title progression for ownership and learning velocity. I am also narrowing my focus from full-stack marketing to product marketing specifically, which closes some CMO paths at companies that want generalist backgrounds.

**Constraints Honored:**
I have two kids under 5 and my spouse travels for work, so relocation is off the table for at least 3 years. Remote-first companies are a requirement. I also cannot take a comp cut greater than 15% given our current financial commitments. My target geography is US-based companies with remote marketing teams.

**Confidence Check:**
This feels right in a way the "stay and get promoted to VP at CloudScale" path does not. The developer tools space genuinely interests me. The honest signal is that I spend my free time reading Hacker News and following developer community discussions, not studying enterprise marketing playbooks. That curiosity is real.

**Accountability:**
I am sharing this blueprint with my executive coach (who I meet biweekly) and my former manager at CloudScale who made a similar transition 4 years ago. I will review milestone progress on the first Sunday of each month and update status. My spouse and I will revisit the financial trade-off conversation quarterly to make sure we are still aligned.
